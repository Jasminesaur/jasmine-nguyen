SALTON SEA

April 21, 2016 - June 7, 2016
The Salton Sea game is a project created in INF 153: Computer-Supported Cooperative Work by a group of 5 students.
Salton Sea Game Password: INF153

THE DESIGN ISSUE The Design Issue: To create a game that helps different stakeholder groups understand and discuss proposed plans for managing the Salton Sea.
The plan was to teach them how to negotiate (in a manner that will promote effective discussion) a plan that could potentially resolve most, if not all, the Salton Sea's problems.
The game is supposed to entice them to keep playing and become more involved within the Salton Sea.

My Role: Research the Salton Sea, put the data together, collaborate on requirements, storyboards, mockups, and evaluations.

The first step was to interview students and research what the Salton Sea was about (by reading articles).
We interviewed an environmental science student and a political science student about their knowledge on the Salton Sea.
For the most part, they knew about the Salton Sea; however, they didn't know about the entire issue.
We gave them an article to read, and they were much more interested about the situation.
When asked if they believed a game could help different stakeholder groups, they thought it was an interesting approach.
The environmental science student wanted to preserve the Salton Sea, but the political science student didn't think the benefits were great enough.
They both insisted that the game would have to be entertaining for the general populace to use it.
We also noticed that the game has the potential networking capability.
One of the interviewees mentioned that the game should have forums, scientific simulations, and a posting of research to be informative.
Lastly, we would have to monitor information being shared to determine what is factual.

THE DATA

The common topics that showed up during research were:
1. Awareness of the Salton Sea: The interviewees had their own knowledge regarding the Salton Sea
2. Potential Game Ideas for Implementation: Player progression can entice people to keep playing
3. Serious Games and Urban Planning: Urban planning has an impact on Serious Games and can bring attention to the community's environment
4. Wilderness: We came across different engineering and alternative solutions

After the interviews, we realized that our stakeholder groups were:
  • Politicians
  • Economists
  • Local Citizens
  • Tourists

    USER REQUIREMENTS/STORYBOARDS

    After brainstorming 20 user needs, it was narrowed down into 4 key requirements.
    1. The game should be educational: The main purpose of the game is to educate users about the Salton Sea's issues.
    2. The game should be affordable/free: The game design aims to reach as much people as possible since it's an environmental message.
    3. The game should be entertaining: The game must be able to draw in users and teach them about the Salton Sea.
    4. The game should allow users to create a profile with basic information: It encourages the stakeholders with participation and interaction.


    After brainstorming 35 design solutions, it was narrowed down into 3 and made into storyboards.
    1. An RPG that will make the player a chosen stakeholder to manage the sea along with a story of the Salton Sea issues.
    2. A simulation game where the players are part of different stakeholder groups, and each is assigned to an area of the sea.
    3. An educational game that explains different stakeholder perspectives to the player and allows them to discern between the pros and cons of each perspective.

    The three design solutions were chosen based on how well it addresses the research problem.
    1. The idea of an RPG provides education, entertainment, and profiles for interaction between users.
    2. The simulation would show how each stakeholder group's solutions to the Salton Sea's issues would compare to others and show which is most effective.
    3. The educational game will mainly provide the educational requirement we identified.




    PROTOTYPE In the end, we decided to combine all three solutions to create the final design:

    A simulation that allows the user to choose different stakeholders and allow them to communicate with other stakeholders in the game about the pros and cons of each perspective.

    The goal is to try and come up with the best solution to deal with the sea.
    Afterward, the players will move forward with the most popular plan, and they will get to enforce that plan within a simulated Salton Sea.
    With the simulation, they will get to watch their plans unfolds on the sea -- for the better or worse.
    Different stakeholder groups will be able to talk to each other about their plans.
    To get the stakeholders to come up with a unified plan, the game will only move forward with the most popular plan.

    EVALUATIONS Lastly, a heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthrough, and two think-alouds were performed.

    1. The heuristic evaluation showed the problems that fit into each heuristic.
    There was a minor usability problem about the user being unable to obtain contextual help.
    2. The cognitive walkthrough showed how the user performs each action within the game.
    The game was simple enough to locate each command and interpret it.
    3. The think aloud evaluations were generally well-received.
    The only problem was about the direction that the player would like to have.

    If I had more time to work on it, I could:
  • Provide better information and direction within the game.
  • Make the voting process clearer and simpler to understand.
  • Turn the prototype into an actual game.
  • Find a way to advertise the game to a larger audience.
